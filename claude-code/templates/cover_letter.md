**[Opening paragraph — Who you are and why THIS company]**

[Opening sentence that shows you've done research — not "I am writing to apply for..."]

Example:
"Your team is building [specific thing from company research] — that's the problem I've been working on since [context]. I'm [Name], a [year] Computer Science student at [University], and I'm applying for the [role] internship starting [month]."

*Purpose: Show specific knowledge of the company in line 1. Name and role come second. Generic openers get skipped.*

---

**[Second paragraph — Most relevant experience]**

[Tell one specific story from your Work History Document that directly matches the role.
Use numbers. Lead with the outcome.]

Example:
"At [Company/Lab], I [specific action] that [outcome with metric]. The challenge was [brief context]. I solved it by [brief method]. The result: [specific number — accuracy, users, time saved, scale]."

*Purpose: One strong story beats a list of responsibilities. Make it impossible for them to forget this candidate.*

---

**[Third paragraph — A project that proves the skill]**

[Describe one project that demonstrates the core skill this role needs.
What you built, why, what stack, what it does, where it lives.]

Example:
"Outside coursework, I built [Project Name] — [one sentence on what it does and why]. Stack: [list]. It's [deployed / open source / award-winning] at [link if applicable]. The most relevant part for this role: [one sentence connecting the project directly to what this team works on]."

*Purpose: Projects are proof. This is the paragraph that makes the difference.*

---

**[Fourth paragraph — Why them specifically]**

[This is not "I admire your company." This is the specific reason this team, problem, or domain.]

Example:
"What draws me specifically to [Company] is [one genuine, specific reason — a technical approach they use, a problem domain you care about, a team member's work you've followed, a recent project that resonated]. I'm not looking for any AI internship — I'm looking to work on [specific thing]."

*Purpose: Shows the candidate chose this company, not just any company. Recruiters feel this.*

---

**[Fifth paragraph — Close]**

[Short, confident, no desperation. State what you're looking for and make one soft ask.]

Example:
"I'm looking for a [duration]-month internship starting [month]. I've attached my CV. I'd welcome the chance to share more — would it be okay to send additional work samples or get on a brief call?"

[Your Name]

## Notes for tailoring

Before sending, verify:
- [ ] Company name spelled correctly throughout
- [ ] Role title matches the listing exactly
- [ ] Opening references something specific — not generic praise
- [ ] Second paragraph numbers are real and accurate
- [ ] Project in paragraph 3 is actually relevant to this role
- [ ] "Why them" paragraph is unique to this company — not reusable
- [ ] Length: 250–400 words maximum. Cut anything that doesn't add information.
