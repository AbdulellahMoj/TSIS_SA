# Question Bank

All discovery questions organised by phase.
Used by the questions agent. Ask adaptively — skip what's already known.
Never ask more than 3 questions at once.

---

## Phase A — Education and Academic Background

1. What made you choose your major?
2. What's your GPA and how do you feel about it relative to your goals?
3. What courses have you found most directly useful for real work?
4. Have you done any research, published anything, or assisted a professor?
5. What's your graduation date and are you on track?
6. Have you taken any online certifications or courses outside your degree?
7. What's the most challenging academic project you've done?
8. Did you take any courses outside your department — statistics, economics, design?
9. Do you have a strong GPA in specific areas (AI, systems, networking)?
10. Have you received any academic awards, honours, or scholarships?

---

## Phase B — Technical Skills and Projects

11. Walk me through the projects on your CV — what did you actually build?
12. For each project: why did you build it, what stack, what was the outcome?
13. Which technical skills are you confident enough to be tested on in an interview?
14. Which frameworks have you used in real projects vs just tutorials or demos?
15. What's the most complex system you've built end to end, solo?
16. Do you have anything deployed, in production, or publicly accessible?
17. What's your GitHub profile like — is there work there you're proud of?
18. Have you contributed to any open source projects?
19. What's the hardest technical problem you've had to debug?
20. How do you learn new technical skills — what's your process?
21. What technologies are you actively learning right now?
22. Which tools or frameworks are you weakest in that you need for your target field?
23. Have you worked with data pipelines, models, APIs, or databases in real projects?
24. Can you describe the architecture of your most significant project?
25. What would you build differently in your biggest project if you started today?

---

## Phase C — Work Experience

For each role listed on the CV:

26. What did you actually do day to day — not the title, the actions?
27. Who did you report to and how much autonomy did you have?
28. What was the biggest challenge you faced in this role?
29. What's the most specific number you can attach to your work here?
30. What did you do that wasn't in your job description?
31. What did you learn that you wouldn't have learned anywhere else?
32. What would you do differently if you could redo this role?
33. Would you go back? Why or why not?
34. What did the people around you think of your work?
35. Did you get any feedback — formal or informal — that stuck with you?

---

## Phase D — Leadership and Extracurriculars

36. Tell me about every club, team, or community you've been involved in.
37. What was your actual role — did you build something, manage people, organise events?
38. How many people were involved and what was the measurable impact?
39. Did you start anything from scratch, or join something existing?
40. What worked and what didn't?
41. What would you have done differently?
42. Did you mentor, teach, or help anyone? What was the outcome?
43. What's the thing you built or changed that you're most proud of?
44. Have you ever had to lead people through a difficult or failing situation?
45. What does your reputation in those communities look like?

---

## Phase E — Hackathons and Competitions

46. Which hackathons or competitions have you joined?
47. What did you build and what was the result?
48. Did you work alone or in a team — what was your specific role?
49. What would you build differently now?
50. Were there any placements, prizes, or recognition?
51. Did you publish or present the work anywhere?
52. How did you handle the time pressure?
53. What's the most ambitious thing you attempted in a competition setting?

---

## Phase F — Career Direction and Goals

54. When you imagine a good day at work, what are you doing?
55. What problems do you genuinely want to solve — not what sounds impressive?
56. Research or product engineering?
57. Startup or established company? Why?
58. Technical depth or technical breadth?
59. Individual contributor or leadership track?
60. What excites you most in tech right now?
61. What's your honest 3-year goal — not a dream, a realistic 3-year picture?
62. Is there a specific person's career trajectory you admire? Why?
63. What kind of work drains you?
64. What environment makes you do your best work?
65. Do you care more about the problem domain or the tech stack?
66. What would make an internship feel like a success at the end of it?

---

## Phase G — Target Companies and Industries

67. Which companies have you been looking at? Say them out loud, even if they feel out of reach.
68. Which Saudi companies do you know well? Which ones are you unsure about?
69. Are there industries you've already ruled out? Why?
70. Who do you know at any of your target companies — even loosely?
71. Have you reached out to anyone at those companies yet?
72. Have you applied anywhere already? What happened?
73. What does your ideal company look like — size, culture, growth stage?
74. Is brand name important to you, or are you more interested in the work?
75. What draws you to [specific company if mentioned]?

---

## Phase H — Constraints and Practical Factors

76. What's your earliest possible start date?
77. How long can you commit to — 2 months, 4 months, 6 months?
78. Full-time or part-time?
79. Remote, hybrid, or on-site?
80. Saudi only, or open to other GCC countries?
81. Open to relocation within Saudi Arabia?
82. Any salary or stipend requirements?
83. Any companies or sectors you absolutely won't consider?
84. Do you have other offers or applications in progress?
85. What's your backup plan if you don't land an internship this cycle?

---

## Phase I — Self-Assessment and Differentiation

86. What's your honest competitive advantage right now versus other CS students?
87. What's the biggest gap between where you are and where you want to be?
88. If a recruiter looked at your CV and passed, what would the reason probably be?
89. What's something about you that your CV doesn't show?
90. What's a skill or quality you have that's hard to put on a CV?
91. What do the people who know your work well say about you?
92. What do you wish you had done differently in the last year?
93. If you could add one thing to your profile before applying, what would it be?
94. What makes you uncomfortable to talk about in an interview?
95. What's the question you'd least want to be asked?

---

## Phase J — Deeper Proof (follow-up on previous answers)

Use these when answers in earlier phases are vague or need substantiation:

96. Can you give me a specific example of that?
97. What was the metric that proved it worked?
98. How did you know it was successful?
99. What would the person you worked with say about your contribution?
100. If I called your supervisor/professor from that role, what would they say?

---

## Follow-Up Triggers

If the candidate says any of the following, push immediately:

| They say | Follow-up |
|---|---|
| "I like AI / ML / data" | What area specifically? Research or engineering? What have you built? |
| "I've done some projects" | Walk me through each one specifically |
| "I have leadership experience" | In what context? How many people? What did you actually do? |
| "I'm good at communicating" | Give me an example where that mattered in a technical context |
| "I work well in teams" | Tell me about a specific team situation that was difficult |
| "I'm a fast learner" | What's the fastest you've ever learned something and applied it? |
| "I'm not sure what I want" | What have you spent time on that felt meaningful? What didn't? |
| "I've been focusing on studies" | What outside class? Any side projects, clubs, competitions? |
